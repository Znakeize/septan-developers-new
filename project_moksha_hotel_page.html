PROJECT OVERVIEW

Moksha Hotel represents a harmonious blend of luxury hospitality and environmental consciousness. This eco-friendly resort showcases our commitment to sustainable architecture while delivering an exceptional guest experience.

The design integrates locally sourced wood and traditional brick construction techniques with modern sustainable systems. Every element has been carefully considered to minimize environmental impact while maximizing comfort and aesthetic appeal.

Natural ventilation systems reduce energy consumption, while open-air spaces create seamless transitions between indoor and outdoor environments. The architecture pays homage to Sri Lanka's coastal heritage while embracing contemporary design principles.

Sustainable Materials

Locally sourced wood, traditional bricks, and recycled materials used throughout the construction to reduce carbon footprint.

Natural Ventilation

Strategic placement of openings and courtyards to harness coastal breezes, reducing air conditioning needs by 60%.

Solar Integration

Photovoltaic panels integrated into the roof design provide 70% of the hotel's energy requirements.

Water Conservation

Rainwater harvesting system and greywater recycling reduce water consumption by 50%.

Green Spaces

Extensive landscaping with native plants creates natural cooling and enhances biodiversity.

Wellness Focus

Design prioritizes natural light, air quality, and connection to nature for enhanced guest wellbeing.
